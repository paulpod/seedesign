Ok, so how are we planning to solve this in context of the secondary market?



Deal ticket for buyers is about entering a quantity, seeing the best (cheapest) shares that are available to complete an order of that size. This may require multiple lots at rising prices, and convert the highest into a partial share lot.

Assumption: we don't need to provide a 'total' as splitting up sharelots (and shares) as required meets the amount entered by the buyer.

Process: share lots get bought up (reserved) by buyers, notification is sent to the sellers, approval results in a cleared-pending sale which gets acted on when the market opens.

  • Why aren't we showing all of the market depth (all the offered shares to sell, in prices all the way up to high/unreasonable?)
  • Would it help anything? What would be the benefit?
  • You could potentially reveal that pricing by putting a large buy quantity in the box
  • the large quantity might try and show all of the available shares/sharelots for sale to fulfill that buy request
